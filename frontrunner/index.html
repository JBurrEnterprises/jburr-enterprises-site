What It Does

FrontRunner Signals identifies key price zones derived from Impulse–Balance–Impulse (IBI) behavior and related candle structure. It helps you focus on the areas where decision-making matters most.

  • Automatically plots supply & demand zones
  • Tracks retouches (how many times price revisits a zone)
  • Assigns a strength score (0–100) and penalizes over-tested zones
  • Optional higher-timeframe IBI zones for added context

FrontRunner Signals is a decision-support tool. It does not place trades or guarantee outcomes.

How It Works (IBI, Plain English)

The core idea is simple:

  • Impulse — price moves hard in one direction
  • Balance — price compresses / pauses (small-body candles)
  • Impulse Away — price leaves the balance area with conviction

That balance area becomes a zone. When price returns to that zone later, it often becomes a key decision point (reaction, continuation, or failure).

How to Use FrontRunner Signals

FrontRunner Signals does not tell you what to buy or sell. It shows you where the market is likely to make important decisions. Most traders use it like this:

  • Start with a higher timeframe (1H / 4H / 1D) to find context
  • Mark the strongest zones and wait for price to return
  • Drop to a lower timeframe to watch reaction and confirm your setup
  • Trade using your own rules (entries, stops, risk management)

Best practice: treat zones as “areas of interest,” then wait for confirmation — not instant entries.

Zone Colors & Labels (What You’re Seeing)

Zone colors

  • Green — Bullish demand / support zone (IBI-style bullish structure)
  • Red — Bearish supply / resistance zone (IBI-style bearish structure)
  • Blue — Bullish neutral / inside-structure zone (context support)
  • Yellow — Bearish neutral / inside-structure zone (context resistance)

Retouch counter + strength score

Zones print a label that may look like:

2 | 78

  • 2 = how many times price has retouched the zone
  • 78 = strength score (0–100)

As retouches increase, strength decreases. Fresh zones are often more meaningful than heavily tested zones.
